The shift the field is watching

Why this is the conversation

For decades, aging looked like a hundred unrelated problems. Then researchers studying the sirtuins, the proteins that govern how cells respond to stress and time, kept hitting the same requirement: sirtuins cannot function without NAD+. Study after study reported the same pattern in aging models: as subjects aged, measured NAD+ declined, and sirtuin activity fell with it. Suddenly a scattered field had a single molecule to point at. That is why NAD+ became the compound the cellular-aging literature can't stop returning to, not because of one dramatic finding, but because it sits underneath so many of them.

Other candidate molecules act on single pathways; the literature positions NAD+ at the intersection of cellular energy and sirtuin-driven stress response. That mechanistic centrality is why researchers treat it as the anchor point, not a footnote.
