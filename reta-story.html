RETA (RT3) — the specifics

What does 'triple-agonist' mean?

It describes the compound's structure. Retatrutide is a single peptide built to engage three receptors studied in metabolic research, GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon, at the same time. A single-agonist engages one receptor; a dual engages two. It is a description of mechanism, not of any outcome.

Why is retatrutide sometimes called 'GLP-3'?

It is an informal field nickname reflecting the sequence of research compounds: GLP-1 (single-agonist), the dual-agonists, and retatrutide's triple-agonist structure. The correct scientific designation is retatrutide, a GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple-receptor agonist.
